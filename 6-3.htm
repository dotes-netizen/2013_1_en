Plan > Overcoming the Bottleneck

1. Today, children do not know the meaning of traditional art. For instance, most of them think dough is clay. However, dough and clay are different, not only by the materials, but also by the process.
Overcoming the misunderstanding: patient explanation and actual practices can help students distinguish the difference.

2. There is always a bottleneck when teaching shadow puppetry and dough. Teach the children the production method and help them use the same method to create different animals and people. Convert the lyric notation to the modern notation.

3. Overcome the difficulties of producing shadow puppetry puppets. In the traditional shadow puppetry, cow skin or animal skin is used for production, but it’s not easy to get in Taiwan. Additionally, it is not easy to operate the tools; it usually takes one to two weeks, or even more, for making a puppet. Now, we use paper instead, for it is translucent and helps to duplicate multiple puppets. We should make the price reasonable and make the puppets popular.

The original animal skin puppets have been improved to paper puppets.
